Can Precipitation Influence Traffic Accidents in NYC?

Overview and Motivation

Precipitation has been the cause of thousands of car accidents year in and year out, and it is especially dangerous in dry weather states.


Something you need to know

In meteorology, precipitation is any product of the condensation of atmospheric water vapor that falls under gravity.The main forms of precipitation include drizzle, rain, sleet, snow, graupel and hail.

The intuition is that heavy precipitation leads to more accidents

There are many factors which may explain the phenomenon.

A Lot of Drivers Don’t Know How to Drive in the Bad Weather

In bad weather conditions, there are two types of drivers who can significantly increase the risk to others: excessively timid drivers and reckless drivers. Excessively timid drivers tend to overestimate the danger of weather conditions and drive at unreasonably low speeds. When the weather limits the number of available traffic lanes, such as can happen following heavy rain, this type of driver can cause traffic to back up, increasing the risk of a rear end accident. Reckless drivers often ignore the consequences of bad weather and may even think it is a good thing that the weather has forced a lot of other drivers off of the road. Reckless drivers may well drive at full highway speed or greater, even when visibility is low or the roads are drenched. Even with four wheel drive (4WD), cars can still skid. When they lose control on a slippery road, their high speed makes it much more likely for a car accident to occur.

Rain Makes the Road Slippery

Rain can make it more difficult to stay on a roadway, to stop, or to avoid colliding with other vehicles. This is especially true when it first starts raining because roads are their most slippery at the beginning of a rainstorm. When the road first gets wet, oil rises to the surface and creates a greasy surface. If you can’t stop your car in time, that’s when accidents happen.

Rain Decreases Visibility

During rain storms, it can be difficult for drivers to see to see other cars. When rain is combined with dirty water kicked up from the road by other drivers, windshields can quickly become dirty. That is why it is always important to make sure that your car has functional windshield wipers.

After those analysis, we have sufficient reasons to believe there should be a strong relationship between precipitation and accidents.

Related Work

When searching for “precipitation and accidents” on the internet, we saw some inconsistent graphs as follows. From the first picture, we can observe that crashes per month is positively correlated with precipitation days per month. In the second picture, the trend of precipitation in Maine represented by the blue line is very similar with the trend of Motorcycle riders killed in transport accident represented by the orange line. The first two pictures tell us there exists a positive relationship between precipitation and accidents. However, in the third picture, the patterns of winter and non-winter weather related fatal collisions are differnt in differnt time periods. The dark gray represents percentage of winter-precipitation-related fatal motor vehicle accidents by hour while the light gray represents the non-winter situation. Sometimes the dark gray bar is higher than the light gray while somestimes the relationship is reversed. This picture tells us weather and accidents may not have a significant correlation.




We became very confused about the relationship and conducted some literature review. In a paper “Weather, Traffic Accidents, and Climate Change” by Benjamin Leard and Kevin Roth(2015), the authors suggested that weather patterns for the end of the century would lead to 603 additional fatalities per year. However, Gráinne McGuire(2008) tried to answer “Does rainfall increase or decrease motor accidents?”, and the analysis in this paper suggested the answer was both. The effect was determined by the quantity of rain and when it actually fell. Although the conclusion was odd, we can obviously know that scholars can not agree on the relationship between precipitation and accidents.
